JEWELLERY QUARTER

Walk around the area that used to contain loads of jewellery factories and still contains plenty of jewellery shops. Admire the intricacy of the Victorian red brickwork, even on simple utilitarian buildings. St Paul's church is very nice. It's regarded as part of the JQ but is a bit out of it. It is close to the restaurant for Thursday evening's meal, so maybe go there before that. The cemetary contains the grave of typographer William Baskerville. There is also a museum.

SOUTH OF CITY CENTRE

SAREHOLE MILL

A 250 year old working watermill famous for its association with author J.R.R Tolkien. Open Wednesday and Thursday, 11am to 4pm - get a tour with a friendly chubby geek.

DAY TRIPS BY TRAIN

Train times and fares: National Rail
  • (NS): Goes from New Street (main station)
  • (SH/MS): Goes from Snow Hill or Moor Street stations.

BLACK COUNTRY LIVING MUSEUM (NS)

Open-air museum of industry and working life. Trains every half hour, take 17 mins, cost £4.10 return
  • Website
  • Map

LICHFIELD (NS)

Pretty town, well worth a full day trip:
  • Huge cathedral containing part of the Staffordshire Hoard
  • Birthplace of Samuel Johnson (of the Dictionary)
  • Birthplace of Erasmus Darsin (grandfather of Charles)
  • Interesting museum of medieval life.
At the north end of the line through New St & University (Litchfield Town, not Trent Valley). Trains every 10 mins, £6.40 return from New St, £8.70 from University

TAMWORTH (NS)

Capital of the Anglo-Saxon kingdom of Mercia. Big castle, nice church. trains approx every half hour, takes <15mins, costs £8.10 return

LEICESTER (NS)

Cathedral with the new tomb of King Richard III, who was found buried under a car park. Trains every half hour, take 50 to 60 mins, costs £14 return

STRATFORD-ON-AVON (SH/MS)

Pretty town. Birthplace of William Shakespeare. Not to be confused with Stratford-atte-Bow in east London, which is mentioned in Chaucer's Canterbury Tales. Trains every half hour, take 40 to 50 mins, costs £8.10.

COVENTRY (NS)

Really quite ugly, because it was badly bombed in WWII and rebuilt in concrete, but the ruins of the bombed cathedral are famous. Frequent trains, take 20 min, costs £5.70 return.

WARWICK (SH/MS)

Medieval castle done up as a tourist attraction. Trains twice an hour, take 30 mins, cost £5.80 return.

BLETCHLEY (NS)

Bletchley Park was where Alan Turing and others broke the Enigma Code. Excellent museum plus the famous huts. Also the National Museum of Computing. Trains take 67 or 92 mins, so this has to be a day trip.

OXFORD (NS)

I think there may be some sort of university there. Trains take a bit over an hour and are quite expensive.

WORCESTER (MS)

Medieval city with cathedral. Frequent trains, take an hour, £9.30 return.

SHREWSBURY (NS)

Very pretty medieval town that can't make up its mind how it should be pronounced. Two trains per hour, takes about an hour, £14.80 return.

IRONBRIDGE (NS)

The birthplace of the Industrial Revolution. Numerous major old factories and museums to see. Probably requires staying nearby for a week. Train takes 34 mins to Telford, then get bus 4.
